# Quick Start

Five minutes, start to finish. This assumes the app is [installed](/guide/installation) and open.

## 1. Search for something

Type a song, an artist, or a half-remembered lyric into the search bar.

Every enabled source answers in parallel and the results merge into one list. A small badge on each row tells you where the track is coming from.

You don't need to pick a source first — that's the whole point.

## 2. Play it

Tap or click a row. Playback starts, and the mini player appears at the bottom.

Tap the mini player to expand it into the full player, where the artwork, scrubber, lyrics and queue live.

## 3. Try the two gestures

These are the ones people miss, and they're the difference between "a search box" and "a music player":

- **Double-tap the artwork** in the full player — left half rewinds 10 seconds, right half jumps forward. Keep tapping and it stacks: `−10 → −20 → −30`.
- **Swipe the mini player** — left for next, right for previous.

Full details on **[The Player](/guide/player)**.

## 4. Import a playlist you already have

Copy any Spotify playlist, album or track link, and paste it **into the search bar**.

The app notices it isn't a search and imports it instead — matching every track against the sources and saving it as a playlist you own. See **[Spotify Import](/guide/spotify-import)**.

## 5. Make it sound right

**Settings → Sound → Equalizer.** Pick a preset, or drag the sliders and the app remembers your curve as *Custom*. Changes are audible immediately — no restart, no re-buffer.

See **[Equalizer](/guide/equalizer)**.

## 6. Download something for offline

Long-press a track (Android) or use the row menu, then **Download**.

Files land tagged with exactly what the player showed you, and play in any other music app. See **[Downloads & Offline](/guide/downloads)**.

## Optional: turn on YouTube

JioSaavn and SoundCloud are on by default. **YouTube is opt-in** — turn it on in **Settings → Sources** if you want rarities, live sets and covers in your results.

On Android the app runs a real self-test on your device before enabling it. See **[Finding Music](/guide/finding-music#turning-on-youtube)**.

---

That's the whole app in six steps. Everything else is detail — and it's all in the sidebar.
