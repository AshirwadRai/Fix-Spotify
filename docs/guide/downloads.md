# Downloads & Offline

Downloads are queued and tagged.

::: warning Playing a song does not save it
Streaming and downloading are separate things. A song you played online is **not**
kept for later — turn your data off and it won't play again.

Nothing is written to your device until you tap **Download**. That's deliberate:
a download is a real file, in your music folder, tagged and permanent, that plays
in any app. A leftover stream would be none of those things — the system can
discard it whenever it needs the space, and you'd have no way to see what was
kept or to rely on it.

**Want a song offline? Download it once. It stays.**
:::

## Tagged with what you actually saw

**A download embeds exactly what the player showed you** — same artist, same album, same artwork.

That sounds obvious until you've used a downloader that doesn't do it. Files land in your music folder already correct, and play properly in any other music player, phone, or car stereo that reads tags.

## Platform differences

| | What happens |
| --- | --- |
| **Windows** | Audio is converted with the bundled FFmpeg. You never install FFmpeg yourself. |
| **Android** | The original container is kept as-is — **no transcode, no quality loss**, and it finishes faster. |

## Where files go

**Settings → Storage** sets the download folder, and there's a reset-to-default if you move it somewhere awkward.

## Watching it happen

A download in progress shows right in the list — a dimmed row with a live progress bar, above the finished tracks, so a queued playlist doesn't just sit there silently until it's done.

## Playing offline

Downloaded songs play with **no connection at all**. They appear in your library alongside everything else.

## Removing downloads

**Hold a song** in Downloaded to start selecting, then tap others to add them — a bar at the top shows how many you've picked, with a shortcut to select every song at once. Confirm, and they're gone from **both** your library and the actual download folder in one step, no need to delete one at a time.

## If a download fails

Occasionally a source will refuse mid-transfer. Retry it; if the track exists on another source the app will generally succeed on the second attempt using a different one.

Persistent failures on one specific track usually mean it only exists on a source you have switched off — try enabling YouTube in **Settings → Sources**.
