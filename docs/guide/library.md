# Your Library

Everything here is stored **on your device**. No account, no sync, no server copy.

## What's in it

- **Playlists** — the ones you make and the ones you [import](/guide/spotify-import).
- **Liked songs** — like from the row menu or from the player.
- **Saved albums and artists.**
- **Downloads** — see [Downloads & Offline](/guide/downloads).

## Pinning

**Pin up to five** playlists or albums to the top of your library.

Long-press a playlist or album and choose **Pin**. Pinned rows sit at the top in the order you pinned them — the first thing you pinned stays first.

Try to pin a sixth and the app tells you the list is full rather than silently doing nothing. Unpin something to make room.

## Track menu

**Long-press a track** (Android) for its menu:

- Add to playlist
- Play next
- Download
- Go to artist
- Remove from this playlist

**Multiple artists on a track?** Tap the artist name and pick which one to open.

## Metadata

Clean names and high-resolution cover art come from the **iTunes Search API** and **MusicBrainz**, so you get a proper artist, album, genre and release date rather than whatever the source filename happened to say.

## Resume

The app reopens on the song and timestamp you left, **paused**, with your queue intact.

## Where it's stored

On your own disk — your PC's app data folder, or your phone's app storage.

::: warning Android: this is why you install over the top
App data lives inside the app's own storage. Uninstalling the APK deletes it, and that's your entire library. Always install a new APK **over** the old one rather than uninstalling first. See [Installation](/guide/installation).
:::
