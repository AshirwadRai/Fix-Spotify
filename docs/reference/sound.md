# Sound & Quality

| Setting | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Streaming bitrate** | Auto · Normal · High · Very High |
| **Crossfade** | 0–12 s — one song melts into the next |
| **Normalize volume** | Evens out loudness so a quiet track doesn't vanish after a loud one |
| **Equalizer** | Eight bands + presets — [full page](/guide/equalizer) |
| **Quality / source badges** | Show or hide the small labels on each row |

## Streaming bitrate

**Auto** picks the best the source offers for your connection. JioSaavn goes up to **320 kbps**; SoundCloud and YouTube vary by upload.

Setting **Very High** doesn't invent quality that isn't there — if a track was uploaded at 128 kbps, that's what you get. It caps the ceiling, not the floor.

Lower settings are worth it on metered mobile data.

## Crossfade

Zero means tracks change cleanly. Anything above it overlaps the tail of one song with the head of the next.

Long crossfades suit continuous listening — DJ sets, ambient, anything mixed. They suit albums badly, where the silence between tracks is often deliberate.

## Normalize volume

Different sources master at different loudness. Without normalisation, a quiet independent upload after a loud commercial single means reaching for the volume every few songs.

Normalisation evens the perceived level so you don't have to.

## Source badges

Each row can show where the track came from and at what quality. Useful while you're learning which sources serve you best; easy to turn off once you've stopped caring.
